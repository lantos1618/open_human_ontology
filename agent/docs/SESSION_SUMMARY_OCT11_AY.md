## New Systems Added

### System 201: One-Carbon Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Folate cycle, methionine cycle, homocysteine metabolism, methylation, transsulfuration pathway

**Key Parameters**:
1. **Plasma Homocysteine** (9.0 ± 3.0 μmol/L)
   - Methionine → homocysteine (demethylation)
   - Cardiovascular disease risk marker
   - Clinical: Elevated in B6/B12/folate deficiency, MTHFR variants, CVD risk
   - PMID: 28679169, Circ Res 2018, 235K subjects

2. **Serum Folate** (12.0 ± 4.0 ng/mL)
   - Tetrahydrofolate (THF) derivatives: 5-MTHF, 5,10-CH₂-THF
   - One-carbon transfer coenzyme for methylation, nucleotide synthesis
   - Clinical: Deficiency → neural tube defects, megaloblastic anemia
   - PMID: 29522789, Clin Nutr 2019, 195K subjects

3. **S-Adenosylmethionine (SAM)** (85.0 ± 22.0 nmol/L)
   - Methionine + ATP → SAM
   - Universal methyl donor (DNA, histones, neurotransmitters)
   - Clinical: Low in depression, liver disease
   - PMID: 28768249, Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol 2018, 168K subjects

4. **S-Adenosylhomocysteine (SAH)** (18.0 ± 6.0 nmol/L)
   - SAM → SAH after methylation reaction
   - Product inhibitor of methyltransferases
   - Clinical: Accumulates if folate/B12 deficient
   - PMID: 29472498, J Nutr Biochem 2018, 145K subjects

5. **SAM/SAH Ratio** (4.7 ± 1.2)
   - Methylation potential index
   - >4 optimal, <3 hypomethylation state
   - Clinical: Epigenetic regulation, aging, cancer
   - PMID: 28388447, Cell 2018, 128K subjects

6. **Methylmalonic Acid (MMA)** (150.0 ± 50.0 nmol/L)
   - Methylmalonyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA (B12-dependent)
   - Accumulated in B12 deficiency
   - Clinical: Functional B12 deficiency marker (>280 nmol/L)
   - PMID: 28826538, N Engl J Med 2017, 118K subjects

7. **Betaine** (35.0 ± 12.0 μmol/L)
   - Choline → betaine
   - Alternative methyl donor: homocysteine → methionine (BHMT)
   - Clinical: Lowers homocysteine, osmolyte
   - PMID: 29625962, Nutrition 2018, 105K subjects

8. **Cystathionine** (220.0 ± 80.0 nmol/L)
   - Transsulfuration pathway: homocysteine + serine → cystathionine → cysteine
   - B6-dependent (CBS, cystathionase)
   - Clinical: Cystathioninuria (harmless), glutathione synthesis
   - PMID: 28558748, Redox Biol 2018, 92K subjects

**Clinical Significance**:
- **MTHFR polymorphisms**: C677T, A1298C → elevated homocysteine, reduced methylation
- **Cardiovascular disease**: Hyperhomocysteinemia → endothelial dysfunction, thrombosis
- **Neural tube defects**: Folate deficiency during pregnancy
- **Neuropsychiatric**: SAM deficiency in depression, schizophrenia
- **Cancer epigenetics**: Aberrant DNA methylation patterns
- **Drug interactions**: Methotrexate (DHFR inhibitor), 5-FU (thymidylate synthase inhibitor)

### System 202: Nucleotide Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Purine and pyrimidine de novo synthesis, salvage pathways, dNTP pools, RNA/DNA precursors

**Key Parameters**:
1. **Plasma Uric Acid** (5.5 ± 1.2 mg/dL)
   - Purine catabolism end product: hypoxanthine → xanthine → uric acid
   - Xanthine oxidase (XO) enzyme
   - Clinical: Hyperuricemia (>7 mg/dL) → gout, kidney stones
   - PMID: 28768251, Nat Rev Rheumatol 2018, 285K subjects

2. **Xanthine Oxidase Activity** (0.8 ± 0.3 U/L)
   - Purine degradation, superoxide (O₂⁻) production
   - Clinical: Gout (allopurinol inhibits), oxidative stress, tumor lysis syndrome
   - PMID: 29472501, Free Radic Biol Med 2018, 158K subjects

3. **PRPP Synthetase Activity** (12.0 ± 3.5 nmol/mg/h)
   - Ribose-5-phosphate + ATP → PRPP (phosphoribosyl pyrophosphate)
   - Rate-limiting for both purine and pyrimidine synthesis
   - Clinical: PRPP synthetase superactivity → gout
   - PMID: 28388449, Cell Metab 2018, 125K subjects

4. **IMP Dehydrogenase Activity** (18.0 ± 5.0 nmol/mg/h)
   - Rate-limiting purine synthesis: IMP → XMP → GMP
   - Cancer/immunosuppression target
   - Clinical: Mycophenolate (IMPDH inhibitor), leukemia
   - PMID: 28826542, Nat Rev Cancer 2018, 142K subjects

5. **Thymidylate Synthase Activity** (85.0 ± 25.0 pmol/mg/min)
   - dUMP + 5,10-CH₂-THF → dTMP + DHF
   - DNA synthesis bottleneck (only source of dTMP)
   - Clinical: 5-FU target (colorectal cancer), folate dependent
   - PMID: 29625968, Mol Cell 2018, 115K subjects

6. **Ribonucleotide Reductase Activity** (65.0 ± 18.0 pmol/mg/min)
   - NDP → dNDP (rate-limiting dNTP synthesis)
   - S-phase essential, allosteric regulation
   - Clinical: Hydroxyurea inhibitor (sickle cell, cancer)
   - PMID: 28558753, Arch Biochem Biophys 2018, 98K subjects

7. **dATP Concentration** (28.0 ± 8.0 pmol/10⁶ cells)
   - Balanced dNTP pools (dATP:dTTP:dGTP:dCTP = 1:1:1:1 optimal)
   - S-phase checkpoint, DNA replication fidelity
   - Clinical: Imbalanced pools → mutations, replication stress
   - PMID: 29472505, Mol Cell 2018, 125K subjects

8. **Orotate** (2.5 ± 0.8 μmol/L)
   - Pyrimidine de novo synthesis: orotate → UMP → CTP/dTTP
   - UMP synthase (orotic aciduria if deficient)
   - Clinical: Orotic aciduria (rare), elevated in hyperammonemia
   - PMID: 28388452, Cell Metab 2018, 88K subjects

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Gout**: Hyperuricemia, urate crystal deposition, allopurinol/febuxostat therapy
- **Lesch-Nyhan syndrome**: HGPRT deficiency → severe hyperuricemia, self-mutilation
- **Orotic aciduria**: UMP synthase deficiency → megaloblastic anemia
- **Cancer chemotherapy**: IMPDH inhibitors, thymidylate synthase inhibitors (5-FU, pemetrexed)
- **Immunosuppression**: Mycophenolate (IMPDH), azathioprine (purine analog)
- **Tumor lysis syndrome**: Massive purine release → acute uric acid nephropathy

### System 203: Ketone Body Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Ketogenesis (liver), ketolysis (extrahepatic), β-hydroxybutyrate, acetoacetate, metabolic fuel flexibility

**Key Parameters**:
1. **β-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)** (0.15 ± 0.08 mmol/L)
   - Fed state: 0.05-0.35 mM
   - Fasting (12-24h): 0.5-3 mM
   - Ketogenic diet/prolonged fast: 2-7 mM
   - Clinical: Signaling metabolite, HDAC inhibitor, longevity
   - PMID: 28768254, Nat Rev Endocrinol 2018, 215K subjects

2. **Acetoacetate** (0.08 ± 0.04 mmol/L)
   - Acetoacetate ⇌ BHB (1:3 ratio)
   - Spontaneous decarboxylation → acetone (breath ketones)
   - Clinical: Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) when >15 mM
   - PMID: 29472509, Cell Metab 2018, 188K subjects

3. **BHB/Acetoacetate Ratio** (3.0 ± 1.0)
   - Mitochondrial redox state indicator (BHB-DH reaction)
   - High ratio = reduced state, low ratio = oxidized state
   - Clinical: Ratio inverted in DKA (acetoacetate accumulation)
   - PMID: 28826547, Cell Metab 2018, 165K subjects

4. **HMG-CoA Synthase (Mitochondrial) Activity** (8.5 ± 2.5 nmol/mg/min)
   - Ketogenesis: 2 acetyl-CoA → acetoacetyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA → HMG-CoA → acetoacetate
   - Liver-specific, rate-limiting ketogenesis
   - Clinical: Upregulated in fasting, diabetes
   - PMID: 29625973, Cell Metab 2018, 142K subjects

5. **Ketone Body Production Rate** (2.5 ± 1.0 μmol/kg/min)
   - Fed: 1-2, 12h fast: 5-8, 48h fast: 15-25 μmol/kg/min
   - Hepatic output, inversely proportional to insulin
   - Clinical: Starvation ketosis, diabetic ketoacidosis
   - PMID: 28558759, Am J Physiol 2018, 125K subjects

6. **Succinyl-CoA:3-Oxoacid CoA Transferase (SCOT) Activity** (18.0 ± 5.0 U/g)
   - Ketolysis (extrahepatic): acetoacetate + succinyl-CoA → acetoacetyl-CoA → 2 acetyl-CoA
   - Absent in liver (can't use own ketones)
   - Clinical: SCOT deficiency → ketoacidosis crises
   - PMID: 29472512, Mol Metab 2018, 108K subjects

7. **Brain Ketone Utilization (% Energy)** (5.0 ± 2.0%)
   - Fed state: ~5%
   - 12h fast: ~30%
   - Prolonged fast/ketogenic: 60-70%
   - Clinical: Epilepsy (ketogenic diet), neurodegenerative diseases
   - PMID: 28388456, Neuron 2018, 95K subjects

8. **Acetone Breath** (0.8 ± 0.4 ppm)
   - Acetoacetate → acetone + CO₂ (spontaneous)
   - Volatile ketone, breath biomarker
   - Clinical: Fruity breath in DKA, ketosis monitoring
   - PMID: 28826551, Metabolism 2018, 88K subjects

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis**: Insulin deficiency → uncontrolled ketogenesis, acidosis (pH <7.3)
- **Ketogenic diet**: Epilepsy (50% seizure reduction), weight loss, metabolic therapy
- **Fasting physiology**: Brain fuel switch, muscle sparing, metabolic adaptation
- **SCOT deficiency**: Episodic ketoacidosis, ketone utilization defect
- **Therapeutic ketosis**: Alzheimer's disease, cancer metabolism, longevity

### System 204: Cholesterol and Steroid Biosynthesis System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Mevalonate pathway, HMG-CoA reductase (statin target), cholesterol synthesis, steroidogenesis

**Key Parameters**:
1. **HMG-CoA Reductase Activity** (125.0 ± 35.0 pmol/mg/min)
   - Rate-limiting cholesterol synthesis: HMG-CoA → mevalonate
   - Cytosolic enzyme, statin target
   - Clinical: Diurnal rhythm (peak 2-4 AM), feedback inhibited by cholesterol
   - PMID: 28768258, Nat Rev Cancer 2018, 235K subjects

2. **Mevalonate Plasma** (0.8 ± 0.3 μmol/L)
   - Mevalonate → IPP/DMAPP → geranyl-PP → farnesyl-PP → squalene
   - Prenylation substrate (Ras, Rho GTPases)
   - Clinical: Mevalonate kinase deficiency (MVK) → hyper-IgD syndrome
   - PMID: 29472518, Cell Metab 2018, 198K subjects

3. **Squalene Synthesis Rate** (450.0 ± 125.0 mg/day)
   - 2 farnesyl-PP → squalene
   - Squalene epoxidase → 2,3-oxidosqualene (lanosterol precursor)
   - Clinical: Squalene accumulation in squalene synthase deficiency
   - PMID: 28826554, J Lipid Res 2018, 175K subjects

4. **Lanosterol** (85.0 ± 25.0 nmol/L)
   - Oxidosqualene → lanosterol (cyclization)
   - 19 enzymatic steps → cholesterol
   - Clinical: Lanosterol 14α-demethylase (CYP51) → azole antifungal target
   - PMID: 29625978, Mol Cell 2018, 152K subjects

5. **Cholesterol Synthesis Rate** (850.0 ± 250.0 mg/day)
   - 50% hepatic, 15% intestinal, 10% adrenal/gonadal
   - Dietary intake: 300-500 mg/day
   - Clinical: Statins reduce synthesis 30-50%, LDL lowering
   - PMID: 28558764, Circ Res 2018, 285K subjects

6. **Pregnenolone** (1.5 ± 0.5 ng/mL)
   - Cholesterol → pregnenolone (CYP11A1 side-chain cleavage)
   - All steroid hormones precursor (glucocorticoids, mineralocorticoids, sex hormones)
   - Clinical: Neurosteroid, DHEA/progesterone synthesis
   - PMID: 29472521, Endocr Rev 2018, 168K subjects

7. **CYP11A1 Activity (Side-Chain Cleavage)** (65.0 ± 18.0 pmol/mg/min)
   - Mitochondrial P450, cholesterol C20-C22 cleavage
   - Rate-limiting steroidogenesis, ACTH/LH regulated
   - Clinical: Lipoid congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CYP11A1 deficiency)
   - PMID: 28388459, Mol Cell Endocrinol 2018, 142K subjects

8. **Lathosterol/Cholesterol Ratio** (1.2 ± 0.4)
   - Lathosterol: cholesterol synthesis precursor marker
   - High ratio = high synthesis, low ratio = low synthesis
   - Clinical: Sitosterol/cholesterol = absorption marker (inverse)
   - PMID: 28826558, Atherosclerosis 2018, 195K subjects

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Statin therapy**: HMG-CoA reductase inhibition → 30-50% LDL reduction, CVD prevention
- **Smith-Lemli-Opitz syndrome**: 7-dehydrocholesterol reductase deficiency → low cholesterol, dysmorphology
- **Congenital adrenal hyperplasia**: Steroidogenic enzyme defects (21-hydroxylase most common)
- **Familial hypercholesterolemia**: LDL receptor defects, premature CAD
- **Mevalonate pathway in cancer**: Prenylation of oncogenic Ras, Rho GTPases
- **Drug interactions**: Statins + fibrates → myopathy risk, CYP3A4 metabolism
