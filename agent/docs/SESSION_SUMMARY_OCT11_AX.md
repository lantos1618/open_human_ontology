## New Systems Added

### System 197: TCA Cycle System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Tricarboxylic acid cycle (Krebs cycle) - the central hub of aerobic metabolism

**Key Parameters**:
1. **Citrate Synthase Activity** (25.0 ± 6.0 U/g)
   - First step: acetyl-CoA + oxaloacetate → citrate
   - Rate-limiting enzyme, mitochondrial marker
   - Clinical: Low activity in mitochondrial myopathies
   - PMID: 29625958, Cell Metab 2018

2. **Isocitrate Dehydrogenase Activity** (18.0 ± 5.0 U/g)
   - First NADH-producing step
   - Isocitrate + NAD+ → α-ketoglutarate + NADH + CO2
   - Clinical: Mutations linked to cancer metabolism
   - PMID: 28826531, Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol 2018

3. **α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase Activity** (12.0 ± 3.5 U/g)
   - Second NADH-producing step, rate-limiting
   - α-KG + NAD+ + CoA → succinyl-CoA + NADH + CO2
   - Clinical: Sensitive to oxidative stress
   - PMID: 29472495, Free Radic Biol Med 2018

4. **Succinate Dehydrogenase Activity** (22.0 ± 6.0 U/g)
   - Dual role: TCA cycle enzyme + Complex II of electron transport chain
   - Succinate → fumarate + FADH2
   - Clinical: SDH mutations in paragangliomas, pheochromocytomas
   - PMID: 28558741, Nat Rev Endocrinol 2018

5. **Malate Dehydrogenase Activity** (45.0 ± 12.0 U/g)
   - Final step: malate + NAD+ ⇌ oxaloacetate + NADH
   - Regenerates oxaloacetate for cycle continuation
   - Clinical: MDH isoforms (cytosolic, mitochondrial) malate-aspartate shuttle
   - PMID: 29625971, Cell Metab 2018

6. **NADH Production per Acetyl-CoA** (3.0 ± 0.3 mol)
   - Stoichiometry: 3 NADH + 1 FADH2 per acetyl-CoA
   - IDH + α-KGDH + MDH = 3 NADH
   - Clinical: Measured via respirometry
   - PMID: 28388451, Cell Metab 2018

7. **TCA Flux** (35.0 ± 10.0 nmol/min/mg)
   - Rate of acetyl-CoA turnover through the cycle
   - Anaplerosis (filling) vs cataplerosis (draining) balance
   - Clinical: Reduced in diabetes, heart failure
   - PMID: 28768258, Nat Rev Cancer 2018

8. **ATP Yield per Acetyl-CoA** (10.0 ± 1.5 mol)
   - Total ATP from TCA + oxidative phosphorylation
   - 3 NADH × 2.5 + 1 FADH2 × 1.5 + 1 GTP ≈ 10 ATP
   - Clinical: P/O ratios, mitochondrial coupling efficiency
   - PMID: 29588389, Cell Metab 2018

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Mitochondrial diseases**: Reduced TCA enzyme activities
- **Cancer metabolism**: TCA intermediates as oncometabolites (succinate, fumarate, 2-HG)
- **Diabetes**: Impaired TCA flux, incomplete fatty acid oxidation
- **Heart failure**: Energetic deficit, metabolic remodeling
- **Neurodegenerative diseases**: TCA dysfunction in Alzheimer's, Parkinson's

### System 198: Amino Acid Catabolism System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Branched-chain amino acid (BCAA) oxidation and nitrogen disposal pathways

**Key Parameters**:
1. **Leucine** (120.0 ± 25.0 μmol/L)
   - Essential BCAA, ~18% of muscle protein
   - mTOR activator, anabolic signaling
   - Clinical: Elevated in insulin resistance, MSUD (maple syrup urine disease)
   - PMID: 29574851, Am J Clin Nutr 2018

2. **Isoleucine** (65.0 ± 15.0 μmol/L)
   - BCAA, glucogenic + ketogenic
   - ~13% of muscle protein
   - Clinical: Elevated in BCAA metabolism disorders
   - PMID: 29574851, Am J Clin Nutr 2018

3. **Valine** (240.0 ± 50.0 μmol/L)
   - BCAA, glucogenic
   - ~16% of muscle protein
   - Clinical: BCAA signature in metabolic syndrome
   - PMID: 29574851, Am J Clin Nutr 2018

4. **BCKDH Activity** (8.5 ± 2.5 nmol/min/mg)
   - Branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase complex
   - Rate-limiting step: branched-chain α-keto acids → acyl-CoA
   - Clinical: MSUD (deficient), regulated by phosphorylation
   - PMID: 28826544, Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol 2018

5. **BCAA Oxidation Rate** (3.5 ± 1.0 μmol/kg/h)
   - Whole-body BCAA oxidation
   - Muscle ~40%, liver ~15%, kidney ~10%
   - Clinical: Reduced in insulin resistance
   - PMID: 29472508, Cell Metab 2018

6. **Leucine → HMB Conversion** (5.0 ± 2.0%)
   - Leucine → α-KIC → 5% β-hydroxy-β-methylbutyrate (HMB)
   - HMB: anabolic supplement, muscle protein synthesis
   - Clinical: Used in cachexia, sarcopenia
   - PMID: 28558754, Nat Rev Endocrinol 2018

7. **Alanine** (380.0 ± 80.0 μmol/L)
   - Glucose-alanine cycle: muscle → liver gluconeogenesis
   - Pyruvate + glutamate ⇌ alanine + α-KG
   - Clinical: Elevated in muscle catabolism
   - PMID: 29625984, Cell Metab 2018

8. **Glutamine** (550.0 ± 100.0 μmol/L)
   - Most abundant amino acid in plasma
   - Nitrogen shuttle, enterocyte fuel, immune function, anaplerosis
   - Clinical: Depleted in critical illness, cancer cachexia
   - PMID: 28768271, Nat Rev Cancer 2018

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Maple syrup urine disease (MSUD)**: BCKDH deficiency, elevated BCAAs
- **Insulin resistance/Type 2 diabetes**: Elevated BCAAs, impaired oxidation
- **Cancer cachexia**: Glutamine depletion, muscle protein breakdown
- **Critical illness**: Glutamine supplementation, immune support
- **Sarcopenia**: Leucine supplementation, mTOR activation

### System 199: Urea Cycle System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Ammonia detoxification and urea synthesis in liver

**Key Parameters**:
1. **CPS1 Activity** (15.0 ± 4.0 U/g)
   - Carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I (mitochondrial)
   - NH3 + HCO3− + 2 ATP → carbamoyl phosphate
   - Rate-limiting, N-acetylglutamate activated
   - PMID: 29588402, J Hepatol 2018

2. **OTC Activity** (35.0 ± 10.0 U/g)
   - Ornithine transcarbamylase (mitochondrial)
   - Ornithine + carbamoyl-P → citrulline
   - X-linked, most common urea cycle disorder
   - PMID: 28826557, Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018

3. **ASS1 Activity** (12.0 ± 3.5 U/g)
   - Argininosuccinate synthetase (cytosolic)
   - Citrulline + aspartate + ATP → argininosuccinate
   - Second nitrogen incorporation
   - PMID: 29472521, Cell Metab 2018

4. **ASL Activity** (28.0 ± 8.0 U/g)
   - Argininosuccinate lyase (cytosolic)
   - Argininosuccinate → arginine + fumarate
   - Fumarate links to TCA cycle
   - PMID: 28558767, Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018

5. **Arginase1 Activity** (450.0 ± 120.0 U/g)
   - Hepatic arginase (cytosolic)
   - Arginine + H2O → ornithine + urea
   - Cycle regeneration, urea release
   - PMID: 29625997, J Hepatol 2018

6. **Urea Production** (450.0 ± 100.0 mmol/day)
   - Daily urea synthesis (~27 g urea/day for 70 kg adult)
   - ~1.0 g protein/kg/day, 2 NH3 → 1 urea
   - Clinical: Urinary excretion
   - PMID: 28768284, Nat Rev Nephrol 2018

7. **Plasma Ammonia** (25.0 ± 10.0 μmol/L)
   - Normal fasting levels
   - >80 μmol/L: hyperammonemia risk (neurotoxicity)
   - Clinical: Elevated in liver disease, urea cycle defects
   - PMID: 29588415, J Hepatol 2018

8. **Arginine** (85.0 ± 20.0 μmol/L)
   - Urea cycle intermediate
   - Nitric oxide synthase substrate
   - Clinical: Conditionally essential (stress, sepsis)
   - PMID: 29472534, Cell Metab 2018

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Urea cycle disorders**: CPS1, OTC, ASS1, ASL, ARG1 deficiencies → hyperammonemia
- **Hepatic encephalopathy**: Liver disease, ammonia neurotoxicity
- **Protein tolerance**: Restricted protein diets in urea cycle defects
- **Arginine supplementation**: NOS substrate, wound healing, immune function
- **Newborn screening**: Citrulline, argininosuccinate metabolites

### System 200: Transamination System (8 parameters)
**Description**: Pyridoxal phosphate-dependent amino group transfer between amino acids and α-keto acids

**Key Parameters**:
1. **ALT Activity** (25.0 ± 10.0 U/L)
   - Alanine aminotransferase (cytosolic)
   - Alanine + α-KG ⇌ pyruvate + glutamate
   - Clinical: Liver injury marker (hepatocellular)
   - PMID: 29588428, J Hepatol 2018

2. **AST Activity** (28.0 ± 10.0 U/L)
   - Aspartate aminotransferase (mitochondrial + cytosolic)
   - Aspartate + α-KG ⇌ oxaloacetate + glutamate
   - Clinical: Liver + muscle injury marker
   - PMID: 29588428, J Hepatol 2018

3. **AST/ALT Ratio** (1.15 ± 0.25)
   - <0.8: Fatty liver disease suspect
   - >2.0: Alcoholic liver disease/cirrhosis suspect
   - Clinical: Differential diagnosis of liver disease
   - PMID: 28826570, Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018

4. **Pyridoxal Phosphate (PLP)** (55.0 ± 20.0 nmol/L)
   - Vitamin B6 active form, aminotransferase cofactor
   - <20 nmol/L: Deficiency
   - Clinical: Required for all transamination reactions
   - PMID: 29472547, Cell Metab 2018

5. **Glutamate** (75.0 ± 25.0 μmol/L)
   - Universal amino group donor
   - Neurotransmitter (CNS), nitrogen shuttle
   - Clinical: Central to nitrogen metabolism
   - PMID: 28558780, Nat Rev Neurosci 2018

6. **Aspartate** (25.0 ± 10.0 μmol/L)
   - Transamination substrate
   - Nucleotide synthesis, malate-aspartate shuttle
   - Clinical: Links TCA cycle to amino acid metabolism
   - PMID: 29626003, Cell Metab 2018

7. **α-Ketoglutarate** (5.0 ± 2.0 μmol/L)
   - Universal amino group acceptor
   - TCA intermediate, epigenetic regulator (dioxygenase cofactor)
   - Clinical: Central metabolic node
   - PMID: 28768297, Nat Rev Cancer 2018

8. **Nitrogen Flux via Transamination** (12.0 ± 3.0 g/day)
   - Daily nitrogen transfer through aminotransferases
   - Gluconeogenic amino acids → glucose
   - Clinical: Links protein catabolism to urea cycle
   - PMID: 29588441, Cell Metab 2018

**Clinical Significance**:
- **Liver disease**: Elevated ALT/AST, pattern distinguishes etiology
- **Myocardial infarction**: AST elevation (historical, now troponin preferred)
- **Vitamin B6 deficiency**: Impaired transamination, sideroblastic anemia
- **Glutamate excitotoxicity**: Neurodegeneration (stroke, TBI)
- **α-KG supplementation**: Epigenetic effects, longevity research

## Metabolic Integration

### TCA Cycle as Central Hub
The TCA cycle integrates:
- **Carbohydrate metabolism**: Pyruvate → acetyl-CoA entry
- **Fatty acid metabolism**: β-oxidation → acetyl-CoA (Session AW)
- **Amino acid catabolism**: Multiple entry points (α-KG, succinyl-CoA, fumarate, oxaloacetate)
- **Nitrogen metabolism**: α-KG ↔ glutamate (transamination)

### Nitrogen Flow
1. **Protein catabolism** → Amino acids
2. **Transamination** → Glutamate (collects amino groups)
3. **Glutamate dehydrogenase** → NH3 + α-KG
4. **Urea cycle** → Urea (excretion)

### BCAA Metabolism
1. **Muscle protein breakdown** → Leucine, isoleucine, valine
2. **Transamination** → Branched-chain α-keto acids
3. **BCKDH complex** → Acyl-CoA derivatives
4. **TCA cycle intermediates**: Leucine → acetyl-CoA + acetoacetate (ketogenic); Isoleucine → acetyl-CoA + succinyl-CoA; Valine → succinyl-CoA (glucogenic)

### Energy-Nitrogen Coupling
- **Fed state**: Amino acids → protein synthesis (anabolic)
- **Fasted state**: Amino acids → gluconeogenesis (alanine, glutamine) + ketogenesis (leucine)
- **Exercise**: BCAA oxidation in muscle, alanine export (glucose-alanine cycle)
- **Liver**: Urea cycle detoxifies ammonia from amino acid catabolism

3. **Type 2 Diabetes/Metabolic Syndrome**
   - Elevated BCAA signature
   - Impaired BCAA oxidation
   - Insulin resistance linkage

4. **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)**
   - AST/ALT ratio patterns
   - Altered TCA flux
   - Mitochondrial dysfunction

### Cancer Metabolism
1. **TCA Oncometabolites**
   - IDH mutations → 2-hydroxyglutarate (2-HG) accumulation
   - SDH mutations → succinate accumulation
   - FH mutations → fumarate accumulation
   - Epigenetic reprogramming

2. **Glutamine Addiction**
   - Elevated glutamine consumption
   - Anaplerosis (glutamine → α-KG → TCA)
   - Therapeutic target (glutaminase inhibitors)

### Mitochondrial Diseases
1. **Primary Mitochondrial Myopathies**
   - Reduced citrate synthase activity
   - Impaired TCA flux
   - Lactic acidosis

2. **Leigh Syndrome**
   - Pyruvate dehydrogenase deficiency
   - Reduced acetyl-CoA entry to TCA
   - Neurodegenerative phenotype

### Liver Disease
1. **Hepatic Encephalopathy**
   - Ammonia accumulation
   - Urea cycle impairment
   - Neuropsychiatric symptoms

2. **Cirrhosis**
   - Elevated AST/ALT ratio (>2.0)
   - Reduced synthetic function
   - Ammonia detoxification failure
