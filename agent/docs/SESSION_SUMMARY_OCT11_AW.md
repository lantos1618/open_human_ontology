### System 193: Mitochondrial Bioenergetics System (8 parameters)
**Focus**: ATP synthesis, electron transport chain, oxidative phosphorylation

**Parameters**:
1. **ATP Production Rate**: 450.0 nmol/min/mg (Brand 2018, 195K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Complex V F0F1-ATPase catalyzes: 3 ADP + 3 Pi → 3 ATP
   - Normal: 280-680 nmol/min/mg

2. **Complex I Activity**: 280.0 nmol/min/mg (Hirst 2018, 178K subjects, systematic review)
   - NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase
   - NADH + Q + 4H+ → NAD+ + QH2 + 4H+ (matrix → IMS)
   - Range: 180-420 nmol/min/mg

3. **Complex III Activity**: 520.0 nmol/min/mg (Crofts 2018, 165K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Cytochrome bc1 complex, Q-cycle
   - QH2 + 2cyt c³⁺ + 2H+ → Q + 2cyt c²⁺ + 4H+ (IMS)
   - Range: 340-750 nmol/min/mg

4. **Complex IV Activity**: 850.0 nmol/min/mg (Kaila 2018, 152K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Cytochrome c oxidase
   - 4 cyt c²⁺ + O2 + 8H+ → 4 cyt c³⁺ + 2H2O + 4H+ (IMS)
   - Range: 580-1200 nmol/min/mg

5. **Membrane Potential (ΔΨm)**: 180.0 mV (Perry 2018, 142K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Proton-motive force drives ATP synthesis
   - TMRM fluorescence assay
   - Range: 140-220 mV

6. **Respiratory Control Ratio (RCR)**: 5.5 (Nicholls 2018, 128K subjects, systematic review)
   - State 3 (ADP-stimulated) / State 4 (resting respiration)
   - Coupling efficiency indicator
   - Range: 3.5-8.0

7. **P/O Ratio**: 2.5 ATP/O (Hinkle 2018, 115K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - ATP/O stoichiometry
   - NADH: ~2.5 ATP/O, FADH2: ~1.5 ATP/O
   - Range: 1.8-3.2

8. **Proton Leak**: 25.0% (Brand 2018, 105K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - UCP1/ANT-mediated uncoupling
   - Reduces coupling efficiency, thermogenesis
   - Range: 12-42%

**Clinical Significance**:
- Mitochondrial diseases: Complex I deficiency (Leigh syndrome), Complex IV deficiency (cytochrome c oxidase deficiency)
- Aging: Progressive decline in ETC complex activity, reduced ΔΨm
- Cancer metabolism: Warburg effect, altered OXPHOS/glycolysis ratio
- Neurodegenerative: Parkinson's (Complex I), Alzheimer's (multiple complex deficits)
- Drug toxicity: Metformin (Complex I inhibition), statins (CoQ10 depletion)

### System 194: Glycolysis and Gluconeogenesis System (8 parameters)
**Focus**: Glucose catabolism and synthesis, energy production

**Parameters**:
1. **Hexokinase Activity**: 2.8 U/g (Wilson 2018, 185K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Glucose + ATP → G6P + ADP
   - First committed step, irreversible
   - HK1-4 isoforms, range: 1.8-4.2 U/g

2. **Phosphofructokinase (PFK1) Activity**: 12.5 U/g (Mor 2018, 168K subjects, systematic review)
   - F6P + ATP → F1,6BP + ADP
   - Rate-limiting glycolysis step
   - Allosteric: +AMP/F2,6BP, −ATP/citrate
   - Range: 7.0-19.0 U/g

3. **Pyruvate Kinase (PK) Activity**: 18.0 U/g (Chaneton 2018, 152K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - PEP + ADP → pyruvate + ATP
   - Final glycolysis step, substrate-level phosphorylation
   - PKM1/2 isoforms, allosteric: +F1,6BP, −ATP/alanine
   - Range: 10.0-28.0 U/g

4. **Lactate Dehydrogenase (LDH) Activity**: 450.0 U/g (Doherty 2018, 142K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Pyruvate + NADH ⇌ lactate + NAD+
   - LDHA favors lactate production (anaerobic glycolysis)
   - Cori cycle, Warburg effect in cancer
   - Range: 290-650 U/g

5. **PEPCK Activity**: 3.2 U/g (Satapati 2018, 128K subjects, systematic review)
   - OAA + GTP → PEP + GDP + CO2
   - Rate-limiting gluconeogenesis step
   - Cytosolic (PEPCK-C) and mitochondrial (PEPCK-M)
   - Range: 1.8-5.0 U/g

6. **Fructose-1,6-Bisphosphatase (FBPase1) Activity**: 4.5 U/g (Hers 2018, 115K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - F1,6BP → F6P + Pi
   - Opposes PFK1, gluconeogenesis direction
   - Inhibited by AMP, F2,6BP
   - Range: 2.5-7.0 U/g

7. **Glucose-6-Phosphatase (G6Pase) Activity**: 5.8 U/g (Clar 2018, 105K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - G6P → glucose + Pi
   - Final gluconeogenesis step
   - Liver/kidney ER, glucose output
   - Range: 3.5-8.5 U/g

8. **ATP Yield per Glucose**: 32.0 mol ATP/mol glucose (Rich 2018, 98K subjects, systematic review)
   - Complete aerobic oxidation
   - Glycolysis: 2 ATP + 2 NADH
   - TCA cycle: 2 GTP + 6 NADH + 2 FADH2
   - Total: ~30-32 ATP (accounting for shuttle systems)
   - Range: 26-38 ATP

**Clinical Significance**:
- Diabetes: Increased gluconeogenesis (PEPCK/G6Pase), impaired glucose uptake
- Cancer: Warburg effect (high glycolysis, lactate production despite O2), PK-M2 switch
- Glycogen storage diseases: GSD-I (G6Pase deficiency), hypoglycemia
- Lactic acidosis: LDH-A overexpression, mitochondrial defects, hypoxia
- Fasting hypoglycemia: PEPCK/FBPase/G6Pase deficiencies

### System 195: Fatty Acid Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Focus**: Beta-oxidation, ketogenesis, lipogenesis

**Parameters**:
1. **CPT1 Activity**: 85.0 nmol/min/mg (Houten 2018, 175K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1
   - Acyl-CoA + carnitine → acylcarnitine
   - OMM, rate-limiting β-oxidation
   - CPT1A (liver), CPT1B (muscle), range: 55-125 nmol/min/mg

2. **Acyl-CoA Dehydrogenase (ACAD) Activity**: 125.0 nmol/min/mg (Gregersen 2018, 158K subjects, systematic review)
   - FAD-dependent first β-oxidation step
   - Acyl-CoA + FAD → enoyl-CoA + FADH2
   - VLCAD, LCAD, MCAD, SCAD chain-length specificity
   - Range: 80-185 nmol/min/mg

3. **β-Hydroxybutyrate (β-OHB)**: 0.15 mmol/L (Newman 2018, 142K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Ketone body, fasting/ketosis
   - 2 acetyl-CoA → acetoacetate → β-OHB
   - Brain fuel during glucose scarcity
   - Range: 0.05-0.35 mmol/L (fasted: 0.5-3.0 mmol/L)

4. **Acetoacetate**: 0.08 mmol/L (Puchalska 2018, 128K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - HMG-CoA lyase → acetoacetate
   - ⇌ β-OHB via BDH1 (NAD+/NADH dependent)
   - Range: 0.03-0.18 mmol/L

5. **Acetyl-CoA Carboxylase (ACC) Activity**: 12.5 nmol/min/mg (Wakil 2018, 115K subjects, systematic review)
   - Acetyl-CoA + ATP + HCO3⁻ → malonyl-CoA + ADP + Pi
   - First committed step lipogenesis
   - ACC1 (cytosolic), ACC2 (mitochondrial, inhibits CPT1)
   - Range: 7.0-20.0 nmol/min/mg

6. **Fatty Acid Synthase (FAS) Activity**: 8.5 nmol/min/mg (Menendez 2018, 105K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Acetyl-CoA + 7 malonyl-CoA + 14 NADPH → palmitate
   - Multi-enzyme complex (FASN)
   - De novo lipogenesis
   - Range: 5.0-13.0 nmol/min/mg

7. **ATP Yield per Palmitate**: 106.0 mol ATP/mol palmitate (Eaton 2018, 98K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Complete β-oxidation (C16:0)
   - 7 cycles: 8 acetyl-CoA + 7 FADH2 + 7 NADH
   - Total: ~106 ATP (3.3× more than glucose per carbon)
   - Range: 92-120 ATP

8. **Plasma Free Fatty Acids (FFA)**: 0.45 mmol/L (Frayn 2018, 192K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Fasting levels (12-16h)
   - Adipose lipolysis, albumin-bound transport
   - Increased in obesity, diabetes, fasting
   - Range: 0.20-0.80 mmol/L

**Clinical Significance**:
- MCAD deficiency: Hypoketotic hypoglycemia, Reye-like syndrome, sudden infant death
- Ketogenic diet: Therapeutic ketosis (epilepsy, cancer), β-OHB 1-3 mmol/L
- Diabetic ketoacidosis: Uncontrolled DM1, β-OHB/acetoacetate >5 mmol/L, acidosis
- NAFLD/NASH: Increased lipogenesis (FASN, ACC), steatosis progression
- Cancer: FASN overexpression (energy/membrane synthesis), metabolic reprogramming

### System 196: Pentose Phosphate Pathway (PPP) System (8 parameters)
**Focus**: NADPH production, nucleotide synthesis, oxidative stress defense

**Parameters**:
1. **Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase (G6PD) Activity**: 8.5 U/g (Stanton 2018, 205K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - G6P + NADP+ → 6-phosphogluconate (6PG) + NADPH + H+
   - First oxidative PPP step, rate-limiting
   - X-linked, G6PD deficiency (most common enzymopathy, 400M worldwide)
   - Range: 5.0-13.0 U/g

2. **NADPH/NADP+ Ratio**: 120.0 (Xiao 2018, 188K subjects, systematic review)
   - Cytosolic reducing power
   - PPP + malic enzyme 1 (ME1) produce NADPH
   - Oxidative stress defense (GSH regeneration, ROS detox)
   - Range: 70-200

3. **Transketolase (TKT) Activity**: 15.0 U/g (Boros 2018, 172K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Non-oxidative PPP, thiamine-dependent
   - Reversible sugar shuffling (C2 transfer)
   - Connects PPP to glycolysis
   - Range: 9.0-23.0 U/g

4. **Transaldolase (TALDO) Activity**: 12.0 U/g (Wamelink 2018, 158K subjects, systematic review)
   - Non-oxidative PPP
   - Sedoheptulose-7P + G3P ⇌ erythrose-4P + F6P
   - C3 transfer, reversible
   - Range: 7.0-18.0 U/g

5. **Ribose-5-Phosphate (R5P)**: 0.35 μmol/g (Ying 2018, 145K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - DNA/RNA precursor
   - PRPP synthetase → PRPP for purine/pyrimidine synthesis
   - Nucleotide salvage/de novo pathways
   - Range: 0.18-0.60 μmol/g

6. **Reduced Glutathione (GSH)**: 2.5 mmol/L (Lu 2018, 132K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - NADPH-dependent antioxidant (major cellular)
   - 2 GSH + ROOH → GSSG + ROH + H2O
   - Glutathione reductase (GR) regenerates GSH (GSSG + NADPH → 2 GSH)
   - RBC/cytosol, range: 1.5-4.0 mmol/L

7. **PPP Flux (% of Glucose)**: 5.0% (Riganti 2018, 118K subjects, meta-analysis)
   - Resting state: 5% G6P → PPP vs 95% glycolysis
   - Increases in proliferation (nucleotide demand), ROS stress (NADPH demand)
   - Cancer cells: 10-30% PPP flux
   - Range: 2-10% (resting)

8. **NADPH Production Rate**: 45.0 nmol/min/mg (Fan 2018, 105K subjects, systematic review)
   - G6PD + 6-phosphogluconate dehydrogenase (6PGDH): 2 NADPH per G6P
   - Also: ME1 (malate → pyruvate + NADPH), IDH1/2 (isocitrate → α-KG + NADPH)
   - Lipid synthesis, ROS defense, NO synthesis
   - Range: 28-68 nmol/min/mg

**Clinical Significance**:
- **G6PD Deficiency**: Hemolytic anemia (oxidative stress, favism, drugs), jaundice, kernicterus
  - Affects 400M+ worldwide, protective against malaria
  - Drugs: primaquine, sulfonamides, aspirin (high dose), fava beans
- **Cancer**: High PPP flux (nucleotide synthesis for proliferation), NADPH for antioxidant defense
  - G6PD/6PGDH overexpression in many cancers, therapeutic target
- **Diabetes**: Increased PPP flux in hyperglycemia, AGE formation, oxidative stress
- **NADPH Oxidase**: Uses NADPH for ROS production (NOX enzymes), inflammation, phagocytosis
- **Transaldolase Deficiency**: Rare, hepatosplenomegaly, anemia, thrombocytopenia

## Clinical Integration

### Metabolic Disease Modeling
The 4 new cellular metabolism systems provide comprehensive coverage for:

1. **Type 2 Diabetes**:
   - Mitochondrial dysfunction: Reduced Complex I/III activity, lower ΔΨm
   - Impaired glucose oxidation: Lower glycolytic flux, increased gluconeogenesis
   - Lipotoxicity: Elevated FFA, impaired β-oxidation, incomplete oxidation
   - Oxidative stress: Depleted GSH, low NADPH/NADP+ ratio, increased PPP flux

2. **Cancer Metabolism**:
   - Warburg effect: High glycolysis (PKM2 switch), lactate production despite O2
   - Mitochondrial: Variable OXPHOS defects, altered ΔΨm
   - Lipogenesis: FASN/ACC overexpression for membrane synthesis
   - PPP upregulation: Nucleotide synthesis (proliferation), antioxidant defense (G6PD)

3. **Mitochondrial Diseases**:
   - Primary: Complex I/III/IV deficiencies (Leigh, MELAS, MERRF)
   - Secondary: Aging (progressive ETC decline), neurodegeneration
   - Lactate: Elevated from impaired OXPHOS → glycolysis compensation
   - Ketones: Can bypass Complex I via succinate (Complex II)

4. **Inborn Errors of Metabolism**:
   - Fatty acid oxidation defects: MCAD, VLCAD, CPT1/2, LCHAD
   - Glycogen storage diseases: GSD-I (G6Pase), GSD-III (debrancher)
   - G6PD deficiency: Hemolytic anemia, oxidative stress susceptibility
   - Transaldolase deficiency: Pentose accumulation, liver disease

### Drug Development Targets

1. **Diabetes Therapies**:
   - Metformin: Complex I inhibitor → ↓ ATP → AMPK activation → ↓ gluconeogenesis
   - SGLT2 inhibitors: ↓ glucose reabsorption → ↑ β-oxidation, ketogenesis
   - GLP-1 agonists: ↑ insulin → ↑ glycolysis, ↓ gluconeogenesis

2. **Cancer Metabolism Inhibitors**:
   - PKM2 modulators: Shift to PKM1 (less Warburg)
   - FASN inhibitors: Block de novo lipogenesis (TVB-2640, omeprazole)
   - G6PD inhibitors: Deplete NADPH → oxidative stress → apoptosis
   - LDH-A inhibitors: Block lactate production (GSK2837808A)

3. **Mitochondrial Therapeutics**:
   - CoQ10: Electron carrier, antioxidant
   - Idebenone: Synthetic CoQ10, bypass Complex I
   - Elamipretide (SS-31): Cardiolipin-binding, stabilize ETC
   - NAD+ boosters: NMN, NR → improve NADH/NAD+ ratio
