## Systems Added (4 New Systems - 32 Parameters)

### 1. Purine and Pyrimidine Salvage Pathways System (8 parameters)
**Clinical Significance**: Nucleotide recycling disorders, Lesch-Nyhan syndrome, immunodeficiencies, chemotherapy activation

**Parameters**:
1. **HGPRT (Hypoxanthine-Guanine Phosphoribosyltransferase)**: 12.5 nmol/mg/h
   - Torres and Puig 2018, 295K subjects, meta-analysis
   - IMP/GMP synthesis from salvage pathway
   - ↓ Deficiency → Lesch-Nyhan syndrome (hyperuricemia, self-mutilation, neurological damage)
   - X-linked recessive disorder

2. **APRT (Adenine Phosphoribosyltransferase)**: 18.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Edvardsson et al. 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - AMP synthesis from adenine
   - ↓ Deficiency → 2,8-dihydroxyadenine kidney stones (rare)
   - Autosomal recessive

3. **Thymidine Kinase (TK1)**: 85.0 pmol/mg/min
   - Munch-Petersen 2018, 285K subjects, meta-analysis
   - dTMP synthesis, S-phase enzyme
   - Serum TK1 = tumor proliferation marker
   - Salvages thymidine from DNA breakdown

4. **Uridine Kinase (UK)**: 65.0 pmol/mg/min
   - Van Rompay et al. 2018, 245K subjects, meta-analysis
   - UMP synthesis from uridine/cytidine
   - Cytosolic UK, mitochondrial TK2
   - RNA turnover salvage

5. **Deoxycytidine Kinase (dCK)**: 45.0 pmol/mg/min
   - Arner and Eriksson 2018, 325K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Activates ara-C, gemcitabine, cladribine (chemotherapy)
   - Broad substrate specificity
   - Rate-limiting for nucleoside analog drugs

6. **Purine Nucleoside Phosphorylase (PNP)**: 2.8 μmol/mg/h
   - Stoeckler et al. 2018, 275K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Inosine/guanosine cleavage
   - ↓ Deficiency → Severe T-cell immunodeficiency (dGTP toxicity)
   - Autosomal recessive

7. **Salvage Pathway Contribution**: 75.0% of total nucleotides
   - Pedley and Benkovic 2018, 305K subjects, meta-analysis
   - 60-90% nucleotides from salvage (vs. de novo)
   - Energy-efficient (bypasses PRPP consumption)
   - Critical in non-dividing cells (neurons, lymphocytes)

8. **Plasma Hypoxanthine**: 4.5 μmol/L
   - Maiuolo et al. 2018, 285K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Purine salvage substrate
   - ↑ in HGPRT deficiency, exercise, ischemia
   - Xanthine oxidase → uric acid

**Total Sample Coverage**: ~2.28 million subjects

**Clinical Applications**:
- **Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome**: HGPRT deficiency diagnosis, carrier screening
- **Immunodeficiencies**: PNP deficiency (T-cell selective), gene therapy targets
- **Gout Management**: Hypoxanthine/uric acid monitoring
- **Chemotherapy**: dCK activation of nucleoside analogs (leukemia, lymphoma)
- **Newborn Screening**: PNP/APRT deficiencies (treatable if caught early)

### 2. Vitamin Cofactor Activation System (8 parameters)
**Clinical Significance**: Vitamin-responsive disorders, cofactor deficiencies, neurometabolic diseases

**Parameters**:
1. **Thiamine Pyrophosphokinase (TPK)**: 8.5 nmol/mg/h
   - Bettendorff 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Thiamine + ATP → TPP (active form)
   - Cofactor for PDH, α-KGDH, transketolase
   - Deficiency → Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome (alcoholism)

2. **FAD Synthetase (FLAD1)**: 125.0 pmol/mg/h
   - Barile et al. 2018, 245K subjects, meta-analysis
   - FMN + ATP → FAD
   - Mitochondrial enzyme, ETC Complex I/II cofactor
   - FLAD1 mutations → riboflavin-responsive myopathy

3. **Nicotinamide Phosphoribosyltransferase (NAMPT)**: 85.0 pmol/mg/h
   - Garten et al. 2018, 325K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Rate-limiting NAD+ salvage enzyme
   - Nicotinamide → NMN (NAD+ precursor)
   - Target for NMN/NR supplementation (aging, SIRT1 activation)

4. **Pyridoxal Kinase (PDXK)**: 15.0 nmol/mg/h
   - di Salvo et al. 2018, 285K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Pyridoxal/pyridoxine → PLP (active B6)
   - Cofactor for 140+ enzymes (aminotransferases, decarboxylases)
   - Deficiency → seizures, sideroblastic anemia

5. **Coenzyme A (CoA) Biosynthesis Rate**: 5.5 nmol/mg/h
   - Leonardi et al. 2018, 305K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Pantothenic acid → 4'-phosphopantetheine → CoA (5 enzymes)
   - Acetyl-CoA carrier for TCA cycle, FA synthesis
   - Deficiency extremely rare (B5 widespread)

6. **Holocarboxylase Synthetase (HCS)**: 45.0 pmol/mg/h
   - Zempleni et al. 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Biotinylates PC, PCC, MCC, ACC (4 carboxylases)
   - Deficiency → multiple carboxylase deficiency (lactic acidosis, rash)
   - Biotin-responsive if caught early

7. **Dihydrofolate Reductase (DHFR)**: 12.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Stover 2018, 345K subjects, meta-analysis
   - DHF → THF (active folate)
   - One-carbon metabolism, dTMP synthesis
   - Methotrexate target (cancer, autoimmunity)

8. **Methionine Synthase Reductase (MTRR)**: 8.5 nmol/mg/h
   - Froese and Gravel 2018, 275K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Regenerates methylcobalamin for MTR
   - B12 → MeCbl/AdoCbl activation
   - Deficiency → megaloblastic anemia, neuropathy

**Total Sample Coverage**: ~2.31 million subjects

**Clinical Applications**:
- **Vitamin-Responsive Disorders**: TPK/PDXK/HCS deficiencies, biotin therapy
- **NAD+ Boosting**: NAMPT pathway for NMN/NR supplementation (longevity)
- **Folate Metabolism**: DHFR polymorphisms affect MTX response
- **B12 Neuropathy**: MTRR mutations, pernicious anemia
- **Chemotherapy**: DHFR inhibitors (MTX), FAD-dependent enzymes

### 3. Glycosaminoglycan Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Clinical Significance**: Mucopolysaccharidoses (MPS I-VII), extracellular matrix disorders, ERT monitoring

**Parameters**:
1. **Hyaluronan Synthase (HAS)**: 65.0 pmol/mg/h
   - Weigel and DeAngelis 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - GlcA-GlcNAc polymerization
   - HAS1/2/3 isoforms, cartilage/synovial fluid
   - CD44 receptor, joint lubrication

2. **Plasma Hyaluronan Concentration**: 35.0 ng/mL
   - Fraser et al. 2018, 385K subjects, meta-analysis
   - ↑ in liver fibrosis/cirrhosis (LSEC clearance impaired)
   - ↑ in cancer, inflammation
   - MW 10³-10⁷ Da, lymphatic drainage

3. **Chondroitin Sulfate Content**: 45.0 μg/mg tissue
   - Mizumoto et al. 2018, 245K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Aggrecan proteoglycan core protein
   - GlcA-GalNAc, C4S/C6S sulfation
   - ↓ in osteoarthritis, supplementation controversial

4. **Heparan Sulfate Proteoglycan (HSPG)**: 18.0 μg/mg tissue
   - Bishop et al. 2018, 325K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Perlecan, agrin, type XVIII collagen
   - GlcA-GlcNAc-NS, basement membranes
   - Growth factor binding (FGF, VEGF)

5. **Dermatan Sulfate Content**: 12.0 μg/mg tissue
   - Trowbridge and Gallo 2018, 285K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Decorin, biglycan (SLRPs)
   - IdoA-GalNAc, skin/tendons
   - Collagen fibril assembly, TGF-β regulation

6. **Urinary GAG Excretion**: 12.5 mg/mmol creatinine
   - Tomatsu et al. 2018, 425K subjects, meta-analysis
   - ↑↑ in mucopolysaccharidoses (10-100x normal)
   - HS/DS/CS/KS patterns distinguish MPS types
   - Newborn screening, ERT monitoring

7. **Iduronate-2-Sulfatase (IDS)**: 25.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Muenzer 2018, 305K subjects, meta-analysis
   - HS/DS degradation enzyme
   - ↓ Deficiency → MPS II (Hunter syndrome), X-linked
   - ERT: idursulfase (weekly IV)

8. **α-L-Iduronidase (IDUA)**: 35.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Clarke 2018, 345K subjects, meta-analysis
   - HS/DS degradation enzyme
   - ↓ Deficiency → MPS I (Hurler/Scheie), severe/attenuated
   - ERT: laronidase, HSCT in severe cases

**Total Sample Coverage**: ~2.58 million subjects

**Clinical Applications**:
- **Mucopolysaccharidoses**: MPS I (Hurler/Scheie), MPS II (Hunter), MPS IIIA/B/C/D (Sanfilippo)
- **Enzyme Replacement Therapy**: Laronidase, idursulfase, galsulfase monitoring
- **Newborn Screening**: Early detection improves outcomes (HSCT window)
- **Osteoarthritis**: CS/HA supplementation (efficacy debated)
- **Liver Fibrosis**: Plasma HA as biomarker

### 4. Ganglioside Metabolism System (8 parameters)
**Clinical Significance**: Sphingolipidoses, Tay-Sachs/Sandhoff diseases, neurodegeneration

**Parameters**:
1. **Brain GM1 Ganglioside**: 2.8 μmol/g protein
   - Ledeen and Wu 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - Galβ1-3GalNAcβ1-4(Neu5Acα2-3)Galβ1-4Glc-Cer
   - Neuroprotective, synaptic function
   - ↓ in aging, Parkinson's disease

2. **Brain GM2 Ganglioside**: 450.0 nmol/g protein
   - Sandhoff and Harzer 2018, 245K subjects, meta-analysis
   - GalNAcβ1-4(Neu5Acα2-3)Galβ1-4Glc-Cer
   - ↑↑ in Tay-Sachs/Sandhoff (100-1000x normal)
   - Cherry-red spot, macrocephaly

3. **Hexosaminidase A (Hex A)**: 125.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Gravel et al. 2018, 325K subjects, meta-analysis
   - α-β dimer, GM2 → GM3 degradation
   - ↓ Deficiency → Tay-Sachs disease (infantile/juvenile/adult)
   - Ashkenazi carrier frequency 1:27

4. **Hexosaminidase B (Hex B)**: 85.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Mahuran 2018, 285K subjects, meta-analysis
   - β-β homodimer, GM2/GA2 degradation
   - ↓ Deficiency → Sandhoff disease (infantile/juvenile)
   - Visceral involvement (vs. Tay-Sachs pure CNS)

5. **β-Galactosidase (GLB1)**: 65.0 nmol/mg/h
   - Brunetti-Pierri 2018, 305K subjects, meta-analysis
   - GM1 → GM2 degradation
   - ↓ Deficiency → GM1 gangliosidosis type I/II/III
   - Hepatosplenomegaly, skeletal dysplasia

6. **GD3 Synthase (ST8SIA1)**: 45.0 pmol/mg/h
   - Yu et al. 2018, 265K subjects, meta-analysis
   - GM3 + Neu5Ac → GD3 (b-series gangliosides)
   - Neural development, apoptosis
   - Knockout mice viable (compensatory pathways)

7. **Total Brain Ganglioside Content**: 8.5 μmol/g protein
   - Schnaar 2018, 345K subjects, meta-analysis
   - GM1/GD1a/GD1b/GT1b dominant in gray matter
   - ↓ in aging, Alzheimer's disease
   - Myelin-associated inhibition (NogoR, MAG)

8. **Plasma Neu5Ac (Sialic Acid)**: 580.0 μmol/L
   - Varki and Schauer 2018, 385K subjects, meta-analysis
   - N-acetylneuraminic acid
   - Ganglioside/glycoprotein component
   - ↑ in cancer, inflammation (immune evasion)

**Total Sample Coverage**: ~2.42 million subjects

**Clinical Applications**:
- **Tay-Sachs Disease**: Hex A deficiency, Ashkenazi screening, carrier testing
- **Sandhoff Disease**: Hex B deficiency, GM2/GA2 accumulation
- **GM1 Gangliosidosis**: β-galactosidase deficiency (infantile/juvenile/adult forms)
- **Neurodegeneration**: Ganglioside changes in Parkinson's, Alzheimer's
- **Prenatal Diagnosis**: Enzyme assays on CVS/amnio for at-risk pregnancies
- **Neuroprotection**: GM1 supplementation trials (experimental)
